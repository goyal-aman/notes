5 Steps for Successful Delegation

Delegating task to co-workers and junior members in team is needed so you can focus on more pressing matters at hand. There are 5 key things communicate every time you delegate task for successful outcome.

  1. Output / end result: Specify what ‘done’ and ‘good’ looks like.
  2. Purpose: (aka ‘Commander’s Intent') What is the point of this task? This gives them a north start to follow when they face decision points.
  3. Inputs: What do they need to succeed? E.g. you'll need this data, read this article, speak to this person.
  4. Constraints: Budget, timeline, and scope are all essential parameters. E.g. you can't spend more than X days; the deliverable must be of Y quality.
  5. Next Steps: Usually this is defining a check-in point, e.g. ‘Schedule 30 minutes for us to review on Thursday afternoon’.
